Short Bio
  • I am currently a Lecturer (equivalent to Assistant Professor in North American universities) in Computer Science at the School of ICT, University of Tasmania (UTAS), and an Adjunct Fellow in the School of Computer Science, University of Technology Sydney (UTS), Australia (2019-). Before joining UTAS, I was a Postdoc Research Fellow in the School of Computer Science at UTS (2017-2019). I received my PhD degree in Information Technology in UTS, under the supervision of Distinguished Prof. Chin-Teng Lin. My research work have been published in top venues, such as ERA & CORE Rank A* level - IEEE T-FS, IEEE T-NNLS, IEEE T-BME, J-BHI, AAMAS, AAAI.

  • My research covers brain-computer interface, computational intelligence, and machine learning for human-centred computing. Currently, I am mainly focusing on the capacity of the human-in-the-loop machine learning and applications. If you are a self-motivated student and keen on a research project to work with me, please drop me an email.

Recruitment
  • I am actively looking for self-motivated Honour, Master, and PhD students who are interested in cognitive AI research topics, including brain-computer interface, reinforcement learning, human-in-the-loop reasoning and learning, and relevant semantic, multimedia, and transportation applications. The programming skill at least one programming language is required (Python, Java, C++, C, and Matlab).
  • If you are interested in doing PhD, please think it very carefully before you commit yourself to PhD. Should you really do PhD?
  • For current UTAS students : Please be advised that I usually take 2 Master students and 1 Honour student each year. Please contact me if you have good GPA and some coding skills.
